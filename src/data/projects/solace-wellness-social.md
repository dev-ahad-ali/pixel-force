## The Challenge

Solace Wellness sold a range of meditation tools, adaptogenic supplements, and wellness journals. They had a great product and a genuine community of advocates, but their social media presence was inconsistent — sporadic posting, no visual cohesion, and zero content strategy. Their 2,400 followers were mostly friends and family.

They needed social media to become a real acquisition channel.

## Our Approach

### Strategy & Positioning

We ran a competitive analysis of 15 wellness brands and identified a content gap: most were posting aspirational lifestyle content, but very few were providing **genuinely educational, practical content** about the science behind wellness practices. This became Solace's positioning.

Content pillars:
1. **Education** — "The science behind [practice/ingredient]"
2. **Community** — User transformations, testimonials, UGC
3. **Product Integration** — Natural, non-pushy product features
4. **Behind the Scenes** — Founder story, sourcing, brand values

### Content System

We built a **monthly content calendar** with a 3:1 educational-to-promotional ratio. Every week included:
- 3 Reels (the primary growth engine)
- 5 carousel posts (high save and share rate)
- Daily Stories (community engagement and conversions)

Reels scripts followed a proven hook-value-CTA structure. We A/B tested hooks continuously, and shared learnings in a weekly Notion report with the founder.

### Influencer Micro-Campaign

Instead of one large influencer partnership, we ran **micro-influencer campaigns** with 20 creators in the wellness niche (10k–80k followers). Each received a product kit and a brief that encouraged authentic, unscripted sharing. Authenticity was non-negotiable — no forced talking points.

The micro-influencer content outperformed every paid ad they'd previously run.

### Social Commerce

We set up **Instagram Shopping** and tagged products in all relevant content. Direct-from-Instagram checkout removed friction from the purchase path. Monthly Instagram Shopping revenue grew from zero to $22,000 by month 9.

## The Result

Nine months into the campaign, Solace had grown from 2,400 to **47,000 followers** with an industry-leading **6.8% engagement rate**. Their Reels regularly reached non-followers, with several crossing 500,000 views organically. Total attributable revenue from social channels reached **$180,000** over the campaign period.

The brand had become a genuine authority voice in the indie wellness space, with other brands reaching out for collaboration.
