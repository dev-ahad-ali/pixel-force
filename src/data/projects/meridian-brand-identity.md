## The Challenge

Meridian Finance was preparing for their seed funding round. Their founding team had deep fintech expertise, but their brand looked like a weekend logo project — mismatched fonts, a generic icon, and no coherent visual language. They were going into rooms with serious investors and needed to look the part.

They came to us with three weeks until their first investor meeting.

## Our Approach

### Discovery & Positioning

Before touching a single design tool, we ran a **brand positioning workshop** with the founders. We needed to understand not just what Meridian did, but what they stood for and who they were speaking to.

Key positioning anchors that emerged:
- **Clarity** — making complex financial data approachable
- **Precision** — accuracy and reliability as core values
- **Forward motion** — a brand that feels like progress, not legacy finance

### Logo Design

We explored three directions in the initial presentation:

1. **Geometric Mark** — an abstract "M" built from precise grid-aligned shapes, suggesting data and structure
2. **Wordmark** — a refined custom letterform emphasizing the "M" and "i" relationship
3. **Symbol + Wordmark** — a standalone icon (a stylized compass rose/meridian line) paired with a clean sans-serif wordmark

The founders immediately gravitated toward Direction 3. The compass metaphor aligned perfectly with their product — helping users navigate financial decisions.

The final mark was refined across **12 iterations**, testing at everything from favicon scale to billboard proportions.

### Color & Typography

**Primary palette:**
- Deep Navy `#0D1B3E` — trust, depth, authority
- Electric Teal `#00C9A7` — innovation, clarity, forward movement
- Off-White `#F5F7FA` — clean, modern, breathing room

**Typography:**
- Headings: *Sohne* — modern geometric with a premium feel
- Body: *Inter* — highly legible at all sizes, excellent for data-dense UI

### Brand Guidelines & Design System

We delivered a **56-page brand guidelines document** covering logo usage, clear space rules, color specifications (HEX, RGB, CMYK, Pantone), typography hierarchy, iconography style, photography direction, and tone of voice guidelines.

A **Figma component library** with 120+ components was handed to their product team as the foundation for the app's design system.

## The Result

Meridian walked into their seed round pitch with complete confidence in their visual presence. The brand was received as a signal of maturity and seriousness. They closed their seed round and came back to us for their Series A materials six months later.

> "People kept asking who did our brand. It opened conversations." — *CEO, Meridian Finance*
