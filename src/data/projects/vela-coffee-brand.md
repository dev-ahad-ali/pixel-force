## The Challenge

Vela Coffee Roasters was roasting exceptional single-origin coffee but selling it in plain kraft bags with a generic label. At farmer's markets, they were consistently outsold by competitors with more visual appeal — despite having a superior product. They knew they needed a brand that could hold its own on a specialty grocery shelf.

They also had ambitions to pitch to café chains and boutique retailers, which required professional packaging and a compelling brand story.

## Our Approach

### Brand Story & Positioning

The founders had a deeply personal story — they had learned to roast coffee while living in Ethiopia for two years, and the name "Vela" (Spanish for "candle") was inspired by the candlelit coffee ceremonies they witnessed there. This story was the brand.

We positioned Vela around **ritual, warmth, and origin traceability** — three values that resonate powerfully in the specialty coffee market.

### Logo Design

The primary logo centered on a **candle flame motif** abstracted into a clean, modern icon — a nod to the name without being literal. The flame shape was also designed to subtly suggest a coffee bean when viewed at certain angles.

Typography was set in a warm humanist serif for the wordmark, contrasted with a clean sans-serif for supporting text. This gave the brand authority while maintaining approachability.

### Packaging System

The packaging system needed to work across multiple SKUs (different origins, roast levels, bag sizes) while maintaining a cohesive shelf presence.

We developed a **modular design system**:
- A consistent structural layout across all bags
- Origin-specific color palettes (warm terra cottas for Ethiopia, deep blues for Guatemala, golden ambers for Colombia)
- Illustrated origin maps on the reverse of each bag
- Roast level indicator using a simple candle height symbol system

All packaging was print-ready in CMYK with spot color options for cost-effective smaller print runs.

### Brand Guidelines

The delivered guidelines covered logo usage, packaging templates, social media visual style, photography direction (warm, natural light; hands handling beans; café environments), and the brand voice — described as *"knowledgeable but never pretentious, like a friend who really knows their coffee."*

## The Result

Within 6 months of the rebrand launch, Vela secured placement in **35 specialty retail stores** across two states. Their DTC Shopify sales increased **156%** as the improved visual brand drove higher conversion from Instagram traffic. Four food and lifestyle publications featured Vela in roundups, citing their packaging as a standout.

The founders report that buyer conversations with retailers shifted entirely after the rebrand — from needing to convince buyers to taste the product, to buyers approaching them based on shelf presence alone.
