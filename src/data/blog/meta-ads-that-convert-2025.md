Meta advertising gets a bad reputation in certain circles. "Facebook is dead." "Organic reach is gone." "The algorithm is broken." We hear it constantly — usually from businesses whose ad accounts are structured poorly and whose creative hasn't been refreshed since 2022.

The reality: Meta's ad platform remains one of the highest-leverage paid acquisition channels available, particularly for businesses targeting consumers or SMB decision-makers. But the game has changed significantly. Here's how we run it in 2025.

## The Mindset Shift: You're Not Buying Clicks, You're Buying Data

The most important reframe for Meta advertising is understanding that your first $1,000–$3,000 in spend isn't about acquiring customers. It's about feeding the algorithm enough signal to know who your customer actually is.

Meta's machine learning is extraordinarily powerful — but it needs data to work. Campaigns that get paused or restructured before they exit the learning phase (typically 50 conversion events per ad set) never have the chance to perform.

The implication: budget matters more than most businesses think. Underfunded campaigns that never exit the learning phase are one of the most common reasons Meta ads "don't work."

## Campaign Structure: Simplicity Wins

The old approach — dozens of ad sets, granular audience targeting, rigid budget allocations — has been largely superseded by Meta's Advantage+ campaign types. Here's our current default structure:

### Top of Funnel: Advantage+ Shopping or Broad Awareness

For most clients, we run one top-of-funnel campaign using **Advantage+ audience targeting** (formerly known as broad targeting). No interest stacks, no detailed demographic layering. We let Meta find the audience.

This sounds counterintuitive. It works because Meta has 15+ years of behavioural data and its audience modelling is — in most cases — better than manual interest targeting.

### Middle of Funnel: Retargeting with Intent Signals

Visitors who have engaged with your content, watched more than 50% of a video, or visited specific pages get a different message. We segment these audiences by intent level:

- **High intent** (product page or pricing page visitors): Direct conversion offer, urgency-based creative
- **Medium intent** (blog readers, video viewers): Value-building content, testimonials, case studies
- **Low intent** (general website visitors): Brand story, social proof, soft CTA

### Bottom of Funnel: Conversion Campaigns

Abandoned cart sequences, limited-time offers, and personalised dynamic ads for users who have shown clear purchase intent. These campaigns typically have the highest ROAS but limited scale — they work only because the top and middle funnels are feeding them.

## Creative Is the New Targeting

Here's the uncomfortable truth that most agencies won't tell you: **creative is now the primary targeting lever on Meta.**

When you upload an ad, Meta analyses its content — the visual, the copy, the audio, the text overlays — and uses that analysis to determine who to show it to. An ad featuring a 35-year-old woman talking to camera about a skincare product will naturally reach a different audience than a product flat-lay with a discount code overlay.

This means:

**Volume matters.** We test a minimum of 6–8 creative variants per campaign and let Meta allocate spend to winners. Single-creative campaigns leave performance on the table.

**Format diversity matters.** Static images, short-form video (under 30 seconds), longer testimonial videos, and carousel ads each reach different users and serve different funnel stages. Running only one format is like fishing with one hook.

**Authenticity outperforms polish.** In almost every A/B test we've run in the last 18 months, lo-fi UGC-style creative (phone-shot, talking-to-camera, real customer testimonials) has outperformed studio-produced content. The feed is noisy. Authentic-looking content doesn't read as an ad.

## Copy Frameworks That Consistently Work

Good ad copy in 2025 follows one of a few battle-tested structures:

**The Problem-Agitate-Solve (PAS) framework:**
> "Still spending hours manually writing client reports? [Agitate the pain]. Your team shouldn't be burning Friday afternoons on admin. [Solve it]. Our AI reporting tool generates them in 90 seconds."

**The Specificity Hook:**
> "We helped a 6-person agency in Manchester generate £340k in new pipeline last quarter. Here's exactly how."

Specificity — real numbers, real timelines, real locations — dramatically outperforms vague benefit claims. "Grow your business" converts far worse than "Book 12 qualified calls per month."

**Social Proof First:**
> "4,700 business owners use [Product] to save 8 hours a week. Here's what they're saying..."

## What to Watch in Your Metrics (And What to Ignore)

**Track these:**
- **Cost Per Result (CPR)** — your actual conversion cost for the action that matters
- **ROAS / MER** — revenue return on ad spend, ideally tracked at account level not campaign level
- **Frequency** — if this climbs above 3–4 for retargeting audiences, refresh your creative
- **Hook Rate** — percentage of people who watch the first 3 seconds of your video (below 25% means your hook needs work)

**Stop obsessing over these:**
- Reach and impressions (vanity unless you're running brand awareness with a specific objective)
- Click-through rate in isolation (a 5% CTR with a 0.5% conversion rate is worse than a 1% CTR with a 3% conversion rate)
- Cost Per Click (optimise for the conversion, not the click)

## The Testing Cadence

We run structured creative tests on a two-week cycle:

1. **Week 1–2:** Launch 6–8 new creative variants with equal budget. Let Meta distribute spend.
2. **End of Week 2:** Kill everything below 50% of the average CPR. Identify the top 1–2 performers.
3. **Week 3–4:** Iterate on winners — change one variable at a time (hook, visual, CTA, offer).
4. **Repeat.**

This process compounds. After three months, you have a library of proven creative elements — hooks, visuals, offers, CTAs — that you know work for your audience. New ads are built from proven parts.

---

Meta advertising rewards patience, structure, and creative volume. Businesses that treat it as a short-term experiment and pull the plug after 30 days have almost always not given the algorithm enough data to find its footing.

Give it proper budget, systematic creative testing, and a full-funnel structure — and it remains one of the most effective customer acquisition channels available.
