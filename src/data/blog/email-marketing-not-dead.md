Every few years, someone writes the "Email Marketing Is Dead" hot take. Every few years, the data proves them wrong.

Email consistently delivers an average ROI of **$36–42 for every $1 spent** — higher than paid search, paid social, or any other digital channel. The businesses that have "tried email and it didn't work" almost always share one of the same five problems. Let's fix them.

## Problem 1: You're Building the Wrong List

A list of 50,000 disengaged subscribers is worth less than a list of 5,000 people who genuinely want to hear from you.

The most common list-building mistakes:

**Buying lists.** This destroys deliverability, violates GDPR and CAN-SPAM, and fills your list with people who have never heard of you. Don't do it.

**Generic lead magnets.** "Sign up for our newsletter" is not a value proposition. Nobody wakes up wanting more newsletters. A specific, high-value lead magnet that solves a real problem — a checklist, a template, a mini-course, a free audit — converts dramatically better.

**No list hygiene.** Keeping unengaged subscribers (people who haven't opened in 6+ months) tanks your deliverability scores. Regularly suppress or remove them.

**The fix:** Build slowly with a specific lead magnet targeted to your ideal customer. Quality always beats quantity.

## Problem 2: Your Welcome Sequence Is One Email

The welcome email is the highest-opened email you will ever send — open rates regularly exceed 50–60%. Most businesses send one generic "Thanks for signing up!" and then drop subscribers into a weekly newsletter they weren't expecting.

A proper welcome sequence does five things over 5–7 emails:

1. **Delivers the lead magnet** and sets expectations for what's coming
2. **Tells your origin story** — why you started, who you serve, what you believe
3. **Builds credibility** — case studies, social proof, results you've achieved
4. **Addresses common objections** — what makes you different, why they should trust you
5. **Makes an offer** — by email 5, the subscriber knows you, trusts you, and is primed to buy

This sequence runs automatically. It works for you every day. Investing 4–6 hours to build it properly pays dividends for years.

## Problem 3: You're Not Segmenting

Sending the same email to your entire list is one of the biggest deliverability and conversion mistakes you can make.

Modern email platforms (Klaviyo, ActiveCampaign, ConvertKit) make segmentation straightforward. At minimum, segment by:

- **Engagement level** — active (opened in 90 days), at-risk (90–180 days), lapsed (180+ days). Send different content and frequency to each.
- **Behaviour** — what links they've clicked, what pages they've visited, what products they've browsed
- **Funnel stage** — leads vs. customers vs. repeat buyers all have different needs and should receive different messages
- **Acquisition source** — someone who joined via a SEO checklist lead magnet has different interests than someone who signed up after watching a webinar

The effort of segmentation pays off immediately in open rates, click rates, and conversions.

## Problem 4: You're Writing for the Archive, Not the Inbox

Most business emails read like press releases — formal, corporate, and clearly written to impress rather than connect.

The best-performing emails read like a message from a knowledgeable friend. They are:

- **Short** — typically 150–300 words for broadcast emails. Long enough to be useful, short enough to be read.
- **Personal** — written in first person, with a specific human voice, not "the team at [Company]"
- **One-idea focused** — one topic, one CTA, one clear next step. Not three announcements and a promotion.
- **Curiosity-driven subject lines** — not descriptive subject lines. "3 things I got wrong about SEO" outperforms "SEO Tips Newsletter — February 2025."

Test plain text emails against HTML templates. For service businesses, plain text almost always wins.

## Problem 5: You're Only Sending Newsletters

Newsletters are one type of email. They are not an email strategy.

The highest-revenue email types are **behaviour-triggered automations** — emails sent automatically based on what a subscriber does or doesn't do. These include:

- **Abandoned cart** — for e-commerce, a 3-email sequence (1 hour, 24 hours, 72 hours) typically recovers 5–15% of abandoned carts
- **Post-purchase** — cross-sell, request a review, introduce the loyalty programme
- **Win-back** — a re-engagement sequence for lapsed subscribers with a compelling offer
- **Browse abandonment** — triggered when a subscriber visits a product page but doesn't add to cart
- **Anniversary** — triggered on the anniversary of a customer's first purchase

These emails run in the background, generating revenue every day without additional effort. For most of our clients, automated flows account for 30–50% of total email revenue.

## The 90-Day Email Overhaul Plan

If you're starting from scratch or rebuilding:

- **Month 1:** Set up a proper lead magnet, rewrite your welcome sequence, clean your list
- **Month 2:** Build your core automation flows (abandoned cart, post-purchase, win-back)
- **Month 3:** Establish a consistent broadcast schedule (1–2 emails per week) with a content calendar

By month 3, you'll have a compounding asset — a list that grows, a system that nurtures, and automations that generate revenue while you sleep.

Email is not dead. For most businesses, it's the most underutilised channel they already own.
